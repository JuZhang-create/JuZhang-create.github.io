Research Overview

My research lies in algebraic graph theory, especially the study of symmetry in graphs via group actions.

Token Graphs

For a graph \(G\), the \(k\)-token graph \(F_k(G)\) is a graph whose vertices correspond to \(k\)-subsets of \(V(G)\). Token graphs encode combinatorial and structural information of the original graph in a higher-dimensional configuration space.

I study automorphism groups of token graphs and investigate when \(\mathrm{Aut}(F_k(G))\) is determined by \(\mathrm{Aut}(G)\). Particular attention is given to Hamming graphs and highly symmetric graphs.

Strongly Regular Graphs

I am interested in structural properties of strongly regular graphs, especially the case \(\mu=2\). My work focuses on clique geometry, complements of strongly regular graphs, and local structures determined by common neighbours.
