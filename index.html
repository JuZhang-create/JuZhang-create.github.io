--- layout: archive title: "Research" permalink: /research/ author_profile: true --- My research lies in algebraic graph theory, especially the study of symmetry in graphs via group actions. ## Token Graphs For a graph $G$, the $k$-token graph $F_k(G)$ is a graph whose vertices correspond to $k$-subsets of $V(G)$. Token graphs encode combinatorial and structural information of the original graph in a higher-dimensional configuration space. I study automorphism groups of token graphs and investigate when $\mathrm{Aut}(F_k(G))$ is determined by $\mathrm{Aut}(G)$. Particular attention is given to Hamming graphs and highly symmetric graphs. ## Strongly Regular Graphs I am interested in structural properties of strongly regular graphs, especially the case $\mu=2$. My work focuses on: - clique geometry - complements of strongly regular graphs - local structures determined by common neighbours - grid graphs $K_n \square K_n$ These problems are closely connected to finite permutation groups and rank-3 group actions. ## Symmetry and Permutation Groups Many combinatorial structures can be understood via group actions. I study how graph symmetries reflect properties of permutation groups, particularly: - vertex-transitive graphs - edge-transitive graphs - automorphism groups of derived graphs
